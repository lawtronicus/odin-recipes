Whiskey Sour

What is a Whiskey Sour?

The Whiskey Sour can be seen as a cousin to the old fashioned and dates back to a similar time period. While the Old Fashioned relies on bitters, the Whiskey Sour combines citrus, usually lemon juice, with sugar, and rye or bourbon as the main spirit.

Two popular cousins of this drink are the Boston Sour, which includes egg white to create foam, and the New York Sour, which includes a float of red wine. Although the inclusion of egg white technically makes the drink a boston sour, it has become so ubiquitous that it is often taken as part of the original Whiskey Sour recipe, and we will include it here.

Ingredients

  • Whiskey (2 oz.) - preferably bourbon or rye, but Irish whiskey or a blended scotch can also work nicely
  • Simple syrup (1/2 ~ 3/4 oz.)
  • Fresh Lemon juice (3/4 ~ 1 oz.)
  • Egg white (optional)

Steps

  1. Add the sugar cube or syrup to the mixing glass.
  2. Add two to six dashes of Angostura bitters.
  3. Add one to two dashes of Orange bitters.
  4. If you are using a sugar cube, muddle the cube with the bitters.
  5. Add your whiskey.
  6. Stir to mix.
  7. Add ice and stir to chill and dilute
  8. Pour over a large rock in a rocks glass.
  9. Enjoy!
